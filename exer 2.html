FOCAL FLOWERS

UNIQUE FOCAL FLOWERS

1. Bird of Paradise (Strelitzia reginae) Description: Resembling the shape of a bird in flight, this tropical flower features vibrant orange and blue hues and an exotic appearance. Unique Feature: Its striking bird-like shape and bold colors.
2. Bleeding Heart (Dicentra spectabilis) Description: Known for its heart-shaped flowers that appear to be “bleeding,” this plant adds a whimsical touch to gardens. Unique Feature: The flowers’ heart shape and delicate pink or white color.
3. Protea (Protea cynaroides) Description: Often called the King Protea, this flower has a large, spiky bloom surrounded by leathery leaves and comes in shades of pink, red, and white. Unique Feature: Its large, artichoke-like bloom and spiky petals.
4. Chocolate Cosmos (Cosmos atrosanguineus) Description: This flower features deep brownish-red petals and has a scent reminiscent of chocolate, adding a sensory dimension to its unique look. Unique Feature: The chocolate scent and deep, dark color.
5. Corpse Flower (Amorphophallus titanum) Description: Known for its enormous size and foul odor, this flower is rare and blooms infrequently. The odor is meant to attract pollinators such as carrion beetles. Unique Feature: Its immense size and strong, unpleasant odor.
